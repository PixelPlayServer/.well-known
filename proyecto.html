WATER CONSERVATION IN OUR SCHOOL

Let's Save Water Together! - A Research Project by Grigori and Joaquin - 8th Grade 2025

üåç Why Water Conservation Matters

Water is one of the most important resources on Earth. Every person needs clean water to live, but many people around the world don't have enough water. In our school, we use water every day for drinking, washing hands, cleaning, and in the bathrooms. However, we have noticed that our school might be wasting water. Students sometimes leave taps running, and some people even block the automatic sensors in the toilets on purpose.

üîç Our Research Method

To understand the water situation in our school, we conducted a survey with 8 students from different grades. We asked them three important questions about water use and conservation in our school.

üí° Our Three Solutions

üìã
SOLUTION 1: WATER AWARENESS CAMPAIGN
What it is: A comprehensive poster and information campaign throughout the school.
  • Create colorful, informative posters with water-saving tips
  • Include before/after photos showing correct vs incorrect water use
  • Place materials in strategic locations: bathrooms, fountains, hallways
  • Update posters monthly with new tips and challenges
Expected results: Students will be more conscious about their water use and remember to turn off taps.
ü§ù
SOLUTION 2: STUDENT WATER MONITOR PROGRAM
What it is: A volunteer program where students help monitor and educate about water use.
  • Recruit responsible students from each grade as "Water Monitors"
  • Train them to educate classmates about proper water use
  • Create a reporting system for broken taps and sensor problems
  • Give monthly awards to classes with best water-saving behavior
Expected results: Fewer incidents of sensor blocking, faster reporting of problems.
üèÜ
SOLUTION 3: SCHOOL-WIDE WATER CHALLENGE
What it is: A fun, competitive program that motivates students to save water.
  • Launch different water-saving challenges each week
  • Track water usage by grade or class if possible
  • Post weekly leaderboards showing which classes are doing best
  • Give prizes to winning classes: extra break time, special privileges
Expected results: Significant reduction in water waste and increased student engagement.
